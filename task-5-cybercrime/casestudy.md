Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails or websites that appear legitimate.

A common example is when a student receives an email that looks like it is from their bank. The email may say that their account will be blocked unless they verify their details. It includes a link that leads to a fake website. When the user enters their details, the attacker steals the information.

Students are often targeted because they may not recognize such scams easily. The consequences include financial loss and identity theft.

Phishing is dangerous because it relies on human mistakes rather than technical flaws. Awareness is the best way to prevent it.
